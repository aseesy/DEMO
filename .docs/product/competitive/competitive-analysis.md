## Market Overview

### Market Size

- ~50% of US marriages end in divorce
- ~1 million divorces per year in the US
- ~40% involve children under 18
- Growing awareness of impact of parental conflict on children
- Increasing court mandates for communication documentation

### Market Segments

| Segment               | Description                           | Size | LiaiZen Focus |
| --------------------- | ------------------------------------- | ---- | ------------- |
| **High-Conflict**     | Active disputes, attorney involvement | 30%  | Primary       |
| **Moderate-Conflict** | Occasional disagreements              | 40%  | Primary       |
| **Low-Conflict**      | Generally cooperative                 | 30%  | Secondary     |
| **Court-Ordered**     | Mandated communication platform       | 10%  | Future        |

## Direct Competitors

### 1. OurFamilyWizard

**Website**: ourfamilywizard.com

| Attribute          | Details                             |
| ------------------ | ----------------------------------- |
| **Founded**        | 2001                                |
| **Pricing**        | $99-149/year per parent             |
| **Primary Market** | Court-ordered, attorney-referred    |
| **Key Strength**   | Legal credibility, court acceptance |

**Features**:

- Messaging with ToneMeter (basic tone analysis)
- Expense tracking and reimbursement
- Calendar and scheduling
- Info Bank (child information storage)
- Journal for documenting observations
- Professional accounts for attorneys/therapists

**Strengths**:

- Long track record (20+ years)
- Court acceptance and attorney trust
- Comprehensive feature set
- Strong documentation capabilities

**Weaknesses**:

- Expensive ($200+/year for both parents)
- Dated UI/UX
- ToneMeter is basic (flags, doesn't coach)
- Perceived as punitive ("court-ordered")
- Complex, steep learning curve

**LiaiZen Opportunity**:

- Modern UX at lower price point
- AI that coaches, not just flags
- Position as proactive choice, not court mandate

## Competitive Threats

### Threat 1: OurFamilyWizard Adds Better AI

**Likelihood**: Medium (they have resources but legacy tech debt)
**Impact**: High
**Mitigation**: Move fast, build AI expertise, focus on UX

### Threat 2: New Entrant with Strong AI

**Likelihood**: Medium (AI startups are everywhere)
**Impact**: High
**Mitigation**: Build brand loyalty, domain expertise, user relationships

### Threat 3: General AI Tools (ChatGPT, etc.)

**Likelihood**: Low (not purpose-built)
**Impact**: Low
**Mitigation**: Purpose-built features, integrated experience, documentation

### Threat 4: Court Mandate for Specific Platform

**Likelihood**: Medium (some courts already mandate OFW)
**Impact**: Medium (limits addressable market)
**Mitigation**: Build court acceptance, attorney relationships

## Opportunity Analysis

### Underserved Needs

1. **Real-time communication coaching** - No one does this well
2. **Proactive conflict prevention** - All competitors are reactive
3. **Learning/improvement** - No one helps users get better over time
4. **Affordable premium experience** - Either expensive or basic
5. **Modern mobile experience** - Most competitors have dated apps

### Market Gaps

1. Users who want help but don't want to feel judged
2. Users who want to improve, not just document
3. Co-parents who aren't in court but struggle
4. Attorneys who want to reduce communication workload
5. Therapists who want tools for clients
