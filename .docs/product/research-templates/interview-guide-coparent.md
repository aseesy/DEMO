# User Interview Guide: Co-Parent Users

**Purpose**: Structured interview protocol for understanding co-parent communication challenges and validating product assumptions.

**Last Updated**: 2025-11-26
**Duration**: 45-60 minutes
**Participants**: Separated/divorced parents with shared custody

---

## Pre-Interview Preparation

### Participant Criteria
- [ ] Currently shares custody of at least one child
- [ ] Communicates with co-parent at least weekly
- [ ] Has been separated/divorced for at least 6 months
- [ ] Willing to discuss communication challenges openly

### Materials Needed
- [ ] Recording device/software (with consent)
- [ ] Note-taking document
- [ ] Prototype/demo (if applicable)
- [ ] Consent form
- [ ] Compensation details (gift card, etc.)

### Interviewer Mindset
- **Listen more than talk** (80/20 rule)
- **Follow curiosity** - dig into surprising answers
- **Stay neutral** - don't lead or validate
- **Be comfortable with silence** - let them think
- **Note emotions** - not just words

---

## Interview Script

### Part 1: Warm-Up (5 minutes)

**Goal**: Build rapport, establish comfort

> "Thank you for taking the time to speak with me today. I'm [name], and I'm working on a product to help co-parents communicate better. I'd love to learn about your experiences.
>
> Before we start, I want to assure you that everything you share is confidential. We're not here to judge - we just want to understand real experiences so we can build something helpful.
>
> Do you have any questions before we begin?"

**Rapport Questions**:
- How old are your children?
- How long have you been co-parenting separately?

---

### Part 2: Current Communication Landscape (10 minutes)

**Goal**: Understand current tools and habits

**Opening**:
> "I'd like to start by understanding how you currently communicate with your co-parent."

**Questions**:

1. **What tools do you use to communicate with your co-parent?**
   - Follow-up: Why those tools?
   - Follow-up: What works well? What doesn't?

2. **How often do you typically communicate?**
   - Follow-up: Is that more or less than you'd like?
   - Follow-up: What drives most of your communication? (scheduling, updates, decisions)

3. **Walk me through the last time you needed to coordinate something with your co-parent.**
   - Follow-up: How did that go?
   - Follow-up: Was that typical or unusual?

4. **If I looked at your message history, what would I see?**
   - Follow-up: Short messages? Long threads? Quick responses?

---

### Part 3: Pain Points Deep Dive (15 minutes)

**Goal**: Uncover real struggles and emotional context

**Opening**:
> "Now I'd like to understand some of the challenges you face. There are no right or wrong answers - I'm just trying to understand your reality."

**Questions**:

5. **Tell me about a recent communication that didn't go well.**
   - Follow-up: What happened? How did it start?
   - Follow-up: How did you feel during that exchange?
   - Follow-up: How did it end? Was it resolved?
   - Follow-up: What would have made it better?

6. **What's the hardest thing about communicating with your co-parent?**
   - Follow-up: Why is that hard?
   - Follow-up: Has it always been that way?
   - Follow-up: What have you tried to improve it?

7. **Are there topics that always seem to lead to conflict?**
   - Follow-up: Can you give me an example?
   - Follow-up: How do you handle those topics now?

8. **How do you feel before you send a message to your co-parent?**
   - Follow-up: Do you draft and re-draft messages?
   - Follow-up: Have you ever deleted a message instead of sending it?
   - Follow-up: Have you ever regretted something you sent?

9. **Has your communication ever affected your relationship with your children?**
   - Follow-up: In what way?
   - Follow-up: How do you feel about that?

---

### Part 4: Workarounds and Solutions (10 minutes)

**Goal**: Understand what they've tried and why it hasn't worked

**Questions**:

10. **What have you tried to improve communication with your co-parent?**
    - Follow-up: Did it work? Why or why not?
    - Follow-up: What would you try if you knew it would work?

11. **Has anyone ever helped you communicate better?** (therapist, attorney, friend)
    - Follow-up: What did they suggest?
    - Follow-up: Was it helpful?

12. **Have you ever used any apps or tools specifically for co-parenting?**
    - Follow-up: Which ones? What did you think?
    - Follow-up: Why did you start/stop using them?

13. **If you could wave a magic wand and fix one thing about co-parenting communication, what would it be?**

---

### Part 5: Jobs-to-be-Done Validation (10 minutes)

**Goal**: Validate or invalidate assumed jobs

**Opening**:
> "I'm going to describe some situations. Tell me how much they resonate with your experience."

**Scenario Questions**:

14. **"I want to send a message without worrying it will start a fight."**
    - How much does this resonate? (1-10)
    - Tell me about a time you felt this way.

15. **"I want to express frustration without being attacked for it."**
    - How much does this resonate? (1-10)
    - Have you ever held back from saying something important?

16. **"I want to find information we discussed without searching through old arguments."**
    - How much does this resonate? (1-10)
    - How do you currently find past information?

17. **"I want to reach agreements instead of endless back-and-forth."**
    - How much does this resonate? (1-10)
    - What gets in the way of reaching agreements?

---

### Part 6: Concept Testing (optional - 5 minutes)

**Goal**: Get reaction to AI mediation concept

**If testing concept**:
> "Let me describe an idea we're exploring and get your reaction."

**Concept Description**:
> "Imagine a messaging app that reads your message before you send it. If it detects language that might escalate conflict - like blame or accusation - it privately suggests a rewrite. Only you see the suggestion. You decide whether to send the original or the rewritten version."

**Questions**:

18. **What's your initial reaction to that idea?**
    - Follow-up: What would make you trust it?
    - Follow-up: What would make you not want to use it?

19. **Would you want your co-parent to use this too?**
    - Follow-up: Why or why not?

20. **What would you worry about with this kind of tool?**

---

### Part 7: Wrap-Up (5 minutes)

**Questions**:

21. **Is there anything else about co-parenting communication that we haven't discussed that you think is important?**

22. **If we built something to help with this, would you be interested in trying it?**
    - Can we follow up with you?

23. **Is there anyone else you know who might be willing to talk to us?**

**Closing**:
> "Thank you so much for sharing your experiences with me. This is incredibly valuable for our work. We're trying to build something that genuinely helps, and conversations like this are how we make sure we get it right."

## Post-Interview Protocol

### Immediately After (within 1 hour)
- [ ] Write down key quotes verbatim
- [ ] Note emotional moments
- [ ] Record surprising insights
- [ ] Identify follow-up questions

### Analysis Framework
For each interview, document:

| Category | Notes |
|----------|-------|
| **Current Tools** | What they use, why |
| **Pain Points** | Top 3 challenges |
| **Emotional State** | How communication makes them feel |
| **Workarounds** | What they've tried |
| **Jobs Validation** | Which jobs resonated (and didn't) |
| **Concept Reaction** | Response to AI mediation |
| **Quotes** | Verbatim memorable quotes |
| **Surprises** | Anything unexpected |

### Synthesis Questions
After 5+ interviews, analyze:
- What patterns emerge across participants?
- What assumptions were validated?
- What assumptions were invalidated?
- What new questions arose?
- What should we test next?

---

## Interview Variations

### High-Conflict Focus
For participants in active high-conflict situations:
- Spend more time on Part 3 (Pain Points)
- Be extra sensitive to emotional responses
- May need shorter interview or breaks

### Low-Conflict Focus
For participants with generally good communication:
- Focus on organizational challenges
- Ask about what's working (to understand success patterns)
- Explore partner inclusion needs

### Attorney Interview
For family law professionals:
- Focus on client patterns they observe
- Ask about documentation needs
- Explore referral scenarios

---

## Common Follow-Up Questions

Keep these in your back pocket:

- "Tell me more about that."
- "How did that make you feel?"
- "What happened next?"
- "Why do you think that is?"
- "Can you give me a specific example?"
- "What would have made that better?"
- "How often does that happen?"
- "What did you do about it?"
